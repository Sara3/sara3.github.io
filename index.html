About Me


Welcome, my name is Sara Daqiq. I am a software engineer based in San Francisco, CA. I love building great products to help businesses succeed in their goals. I appreciate a sleek and fast user experience, and will work tirelessly to find solutions to challenging engineering problems. In my most recent application Flashback, I used React for fast rendering and used Redux for efficient data management resulting in seamless user experience. I’ve also built a smart mirror that integrated Slack, Google Calendar, Periscope Data, and Weather using APIs to render useful data.

I am passionate about women’s rights. I have a huge interest in helping women get financially independent, and work with women around the world to help them develop skills that enable them to achieve their dreams. In 2012, I founded a company, AccessLocal, that teaches underprivileged females in rural Afghanistan literacy and financial planning. Likewise, I’ve also served two summers as a Girls Who Code Computer Science teacher, sharing CS fundamentals and web design skills with high-school girls.

Most recently, I’ve particularly enjoyed diving into the world of bots and progressive web applications recently. One of my most recent applications is a Facebook Messenger bot that responds to questions with tweets from Donald Trump, and my application Flashback, was built as progressive web app. I have a strong background in Javascript and Node.js, and I’ve worked on applications built with ES6, React, Angular, PostgreSQL, MySQL, Knex, and Express. With every application, I aim to produce a modular and well-documented codebase that is both scalable and maintainable, and it brings me great joy to be able to build, improve on, and implement products that I’m proud of.
